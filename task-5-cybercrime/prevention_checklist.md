# Stay Safe Online Checklist:



1)Verify UPI IDs

Always cross-check the UPI ID before making a payment. If the ID seems suspicious or the name of the ID seems fake, stop the payment.



2)Avoid unknown payment links: 

Do not click on suspicious links or enter payment details on unknown websites. Always check the website URL before proceeding with any transactions. You can use a scam advisor like https://www.scamadviser.com/ to check for public review and rating of the website.



3)Use verified apps: 

Always rely on verified apps like Google Pay, Paytm, and PhonePe while making transactions. 



4)Stay away from websites distributing pirated Movies , Games or other Software:

Always use legitimate website and services for better security, these fake website often plant dangerous cookies ( local website code that runs on your device , it is typically used to load common data and execute website services feasible directly on your device through your web browser)



5)Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Adding 2FA to your accounts provides an extra layer of security. Even after a password is compromised, a second verification step—like a code from an authenticator app—prevents unauthorized access. Avoid relying solely on SMS codes, as they can be intercepted.



6)Stay alert for phishing emails :

Common red flags include generic greetings, poor grammar, urgent requests, unusual links, unexpected attachments, and offers that seem too good to be true. Always verify the sender’s email address and avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown or suspicious sources.



7)Stay aware of fake websites which replicate original:

always check https: in name of website link. Be vigilant if you spot some poor grammar of a famous website , website is taking a long time to respond , minor change in website name or any other abnormalities . Websites are usually kept consistent for trust and developer ease of debugging.



8)Use an antivirus and a password manger with 2FA :

use antivirus on important devices and always set password with a password manager if you have many password as it is hard to keep in memory a lot of different passwords. Always use a reputable password managers like Proton Pass , Bitwarden , 1password and many more which you can find on google with 2FA in case you forget password to your password managers.



9)In case of any cyber-fraud or a suspect of Fraud report the situation to cyber crime department of Indian government www.cybercrime.gov.in :

Use the 'Report Suspect' facility to report identifiers such as website URLs, phone numbers, email IDs, and more.

Fill out the online complaint form with accurate details and upload supporting evidence.

Submit your complaint and check the real-time status using your complaint ID and registered mobile number.

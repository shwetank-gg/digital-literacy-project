# 5 DO’S OF SOCIAL MEDIA



#### 1\. Build a Professional LinkedIn Profile

\* Use a clear, professional headshot.

\* Highlight your education, internships, projects, and technical skills.

\* Write concise descriptions of achievements rather than just listing duties.

\* Regularly update your profile to reflect new experiences. 

#### 2\. Engage in Thoughtful Networking

\* Connect with professors, alumni, and professionals in your field.

\* Comment on industry posts with insights to show engagement.

\* Join groups related to your career interests to stay updated.

#### 3\. Share Relevant Content

\* Post about academic projects, research, or events you attend.

\* Share articles or news related to your field with short reflections.

\* This demonstrates curiosity and initiative to potential employers.

#### 4\. Maintain a Clean Online Presence

\* Audit your Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok for posts that may appear unprofessional.

\* Employers often Google candidates; ensure your public content reflects maturity. 

#### 5\. Use Social Media for Skill Development

\* Follow industry leaders, companies, and educational pages.

\* Engage with tutorials, webinars, and online communities to learn new tools.

\* Document your learning journey to show growth and adaptability.

# 5 DON’T OF SOCIAL MEDIA

#### 

#### 1\. Don’t Post Inappropriate Content

o Avoid offensive jokes, political rants, or photos that could be seen as irresponsible.

o Even old posts can resurface and damage credibility. 

#### 2\. Don’t Overshare Personal Life

o Excessive personal details can distract from your professional image.

o Keep private updates limited to close friends, not public feeds.

#### 3\. Don’t Ignore Privacy Settings

o Review who can see your posts and tagged photos.

o Restrict visibility of casual content to friends only.

#### 4\. Don’t Spam Connections

o Avoid sending mass connection requests or posting too frequently.

o Quality engagement is more valuable than quantity.

#### 5\. Don’t Neglect Consistency

o Inactive or outdated profiles suggest lack of initiative.

o If you create a professional account, maintain it regularly.
